# F. Glossary Terms — Full Set (Alphabetical)

### `/glossary/24-hour-rule`
**24-Hour Rule**

The contemporary expectation that material reputation events require a substantive public response within 24 hours — replacing the older 72-hour disclosure window.

The compression from 72 hours to 24 has been driven by social media velocity, regulatory tightening, and AI summarization. Companies that meet the 24-hour expectation with disciplined, specific, factual disclosure shape the narrative; companies that miss it get framed by critics, short-sellers, and adversaries. The rule applies across breach response, recalls, executive misconduct, and regulatory enforcement.

**Where it's used:** [Crisis Communications](/crisis-communications)
**Related terms:** [Crisis Velocity](/glossary/crisis-velocity), [Disclosure Quality](/glossary/disclosure-quality)

### `/glossary/activist-short-seller`
**Activist Short-Seller**

An investor who takes a short position in a public company and then publishes a report alleging fraud, accounting irregularities, or operational failures — intending to drive the stock down.

Activist short-seller campaigns operate as communications attacks as much as financial attacks. Leading firms include Muddy Waters, Spruce Point, and Kerrisdale Capital. Defending against an activist short report is a specialized crisis discipline combining investor relations, earned media, and forensic financial communications.

**Where it's used:** [Crisis Communications](/crisis-communications), [Investor Relations](/investor-relations)
**Related terms:** [Reputation Recovery](/glossary/reputation-recovery)

### `/glossary/aeo`
**AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)**

The practice of structuring content, data, and authority signals to win direct-answer surfaces inside search engines and assistants.

AEO targets featured snippets, Google AI Overviews, voice search responses, and knowledge panels — the surfaces that resolve a query without a click-through. It overlaps with [SEO](/glossary/seo) and [GEO](/glossary/geo) but draws on different signals: AEO weights structured data, question-led content architecture, and schema markup heavily. The three disciplines reinforce each other when coordinated and undermine each other when run in silos.

**Where it's used:** [AEO](/aeo), [SEO](/seo), [AI Communications & GEO](/ai)
**Related terms:** [GEO](/glossary/geo), [SEO](/glossary/seo), [AI Overview](/glossary/ai-overview), [Zero-Click Search](/glossary/zero-click-search)

### `/glossary/ai-disclosure`
**AI Disclosure**

A public statement — by a company, financial institution, healthcare provider, or platform — describing how AI is used in its products, services, or operations.

AI disclosure has emerged as a reputation variable across financial services, healthcare, legal, and consumer categories. Audits of top U.S. banks and wealth firms show meaningful gaps in transparency around AI in advisory, underwriting, and fraud detection — and those gaps shape how the brands appear inside generative search. SEC disclosure guidance and model governance frameworks now influence how AI disclosure quality affects investor and customer perception.

**Where it's used:** [Financial Services & Fintech](/finserv), [Healthcare & Health Tech](/healthcare), [AI Communications & GEO](/ai)
**Related terms:** [Disclosure Quality](/glossary/disclosure-quality), [SEC 8-K](/glossary/sec-8k)

### `/glossary/ai-overview`
**AI Overview**

The summarized AI-generated answer that appears at the top of Google search results for many queries — replacing or sitting above the traditional ranked-link list.

Google AI Overviews have reshaped organic traffic patterns across information-led categories. For users, the Overview often resolves the query without a click; for brands, the Overview's source citations have become a primary discovery surface. AI Overviews pull from a broader source mix than ranked SEO results, including structured data, schema-marked content, and entity-authoritative references.

**Where it's used:** [AEO](/aeo), [SEO](/seo), [AI Communications & GEO](/ai)
**Related terms:** [AEO](/glossary/aeo), [Zero-Click Search](/glossary/zero-click-search), [Featured Snippet](/glossary/featured-snippet)

### `/glossary/ai-scribe`
**AI Scribe**

Software that uses AI — typically large language models combined with speech recognition — to listen to physician-patient encounters and automatically generate clinical documentation.

Leading AI scribe platforms include Abridge, Nuance DAX, Suki, DeepScribe, and Microsoft Dragon Copilot. AI scribes have reshaped the physician-technology relationship faster than almost any other healthcare AI application — primarily because they relieve documentation burden, the single largest driver of physician burnout.

**Where it's used:** [Healthcare & Health Tech](/healthcare)
**Related terms:** [Payor-Provider](/glossary/payor-provider)

### `/glossary/ai-underwriting`
**AI Underwriting**

The use of AI models to assess credit, insurance, or lending risk — often in place of or alongside traditional human underwriting and conventional credit-scoring methods.

AI underwriting is reshaping credit, insurance, and KYC across every layer of financial services. The category creates new disclosure obligations — what data the model uses, what features it weights, what bias-testing is in place — and new reputation exposure when the model produces controversial outcomes.

**Where it's used:** [Financial Services & Fintech](/finserv), [AI Communications & GEO](/ai)
**Related terms:** [AI Disclosure](/glossary/ai-disclosure), [KYC](/glossary/kyc)

### `/glossary/amlaw-100`
**AmLaw 100**

The annual ranking of the 100 largest U.S. law firms by revenue, published by The American Lawyer.

AmLaw 100 status is a primary competitive and recruiting signal for major law firms. Movement within the rankings — and the partner moves, practice launches, and M&A driving that movement — generates substantial trade press coverage. AmLaw firms compete for lateral partners, corporate clients, and Tier-1 press placement in markets defined by reputation as much as expertise.

**Where it's used:** [Legal & Litigation Communications](/legal)

### `/glossary/analyst-relations`
**Analyst Relations**

The structured engagement between B2B technology companies and industry analyst firms — including Gartner, Forrester, IDC, ISG, and 451 Research.

Analyst relations shapes how a B2B company appears in influential reports (Magic Quadrant, Wave, MarketScape), how analysts brief enterprise buyers, and how the buying committee evaluates the vendor. Strong analyst relations compounds across procurement cycles. The discipline now operates alongside AI-mediated buyer research — analyst reports themselves feed AI engines.

**Where it's used:** [B2B Tech & SaaS](/b2b)
**Related terms:** [Category Creation](/glossary/category-creation), [Procurement](/glossary/procurement)

### `/glossary/aor`
**AOR (Agency of Record)**

A formal designation that a single agency holds the primary relationship with a brand or division for a defined scope of work — public relations, advertising, media buying, or digital.

AOR assignments are central competitive events in the agency industry. They are typically awarded through an RFP-driven review and govern multi-year relationships worth millions. AOR reviews generate trade press, drive lateral movement of agency talent, and reshape the competitive landscape.

**Where it's used:** [Earned Media](/earned-media), [AdTech & MarTech](/adtech)
**Related terms:** [RFP](/glossary/rfp), [Procurement](/glossary/procurement)

### `/glossary/boilerplate`
**Boilerplate**

The standardized block of corporate copy that appears at the end of every press release — typically containing the company's one-paragraph description, key facts, and contact information.

Boilerplate matters more than it used to. It now feeds AI engines a consistent, structured corporate summary across every press release a company issues — which means a well-written, entity-rich boilerplate compounds into AI citation authority over hundreds of releases.

**Where it's used:** [Earned Media](/earned-media), [GEO](/geo)
**Related terms:** [Press Release](/glossary/press-release), [Entity Authority](/glossary/entity-authority)

### `/glossary/breach-response`
**Breach Response**

The communications, regulatory, and operational response to a cybersecurity incident — covering regulatory disclosure, customer notification, employee communications, and post-incident reputation management.

Breach response operates under intensified time pressure since the SEC's 2023 cyber disclosure rules. Material breaches must be disclosed via Form 8-K within four business days. Research shows disclosure quality drives stock recovery more than incident severity — meaning how a breach is communicated now matters as much as what happened.

**Where it's used:** [Cybersecurity](/cyber), [Crisis Communications](/crisis-communications)
**Related terms:** [SEC 8-K](/glossary/sec-8k), [Disclosure Quality](/glossary/disclosure-quality), [Crisis Velocity](/glossary/crisis-velocity)

### `/glossary/category-creation`
**Category Creation**

The strategic and communications work of defining a new market category — naming it, framing the problem it solves, and establishing the company as the category leader.

Category creation requires sustained earned media, analyst engagement, founder branding, and customer advocacy. Successful examples include HubSpot creating "inbound marketing," Drift creating "conversational marketing," and Gong creating "revenue intelligence." Category creation now also requires AI visibility — the category name has to surface inside AI answers, and the company has to be the named entity associated with it.

**Where it's used:** [B2B Tech & SaaS](/b2b)
**Related terms:** [Analyst Relations](/glossary/analyst-relations), [Founder Branding](/glossary/founder-branding), [Entity Authority](/glossary/entity-authority)

### `/glossary/cdp`
**CDP (Customer Data Platform)**

A unified system that combines customer data from multiple sources — website, app, email, point-of-sale, support, ad platforms — into a single persistent profile usable across marketing, analytics, and customer experience.

CDPs became essential infrastructure as signal loss compressed the value of third-party data. The leading enterprise CDPs include Segment (Twilio), Salesforce Data Cloud, Adobe Real-Time CDP, Tealium, and Treasure Data. CDP buying has become procurement-led across most enterprises.

**Where it's used:** [AdTech & MarTech](/adtech), [B2B Tech & SaaS](/b2b)
**Related terms:** [Signal Loss](/glossary/signal-loss), [Clean Room](/glossary/clean-room), [Walled Gardens](/glossary/walled-gardens)

### `/glossary/citation-share`
**Citation Share**

The percentage of generative AI answers about a category in which a given brand is cited as a source, named option, or recommendation.

Citation share is the GEO equivalent of share of voice. It is measured through structured prompt testing across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews — typically using a controlled prompt set per category at consistent intervals. A brand with 35% citation share in "best CDP for enterprise" queries appears in roughly one out of three Claude or ChatGPT answers about enterprise CDPs.

**Where it's used:** [AI Communications & GEO](/ai), [GEO](/geo)
**Related terms:** [GEO](/glossary/geo), [Share of Model](/glossary/share-of-model), [Retrieval Anchor](/glossary/retrieval-anchor)

### `/glossary/clean-room`
**Clean Room**

A secure data environment where two or more parties — typically a brand and a media platform or retailer — can analyze combined datasets without exposing the underlying user-level information.

Clean rooms emerged as a structural response to signal loss. They allow advertisers to measure campaign performance, build lookalike audiences, and run analytics on retail media networks without violating privacy regulation or sharing raw customer data. Leading platforms include Amazon Marketing Cloud, Disney's Clean Room, LiveRamp, Snowflake, and Habu.

**Where it's used:** [AdTech & MarTech](/adtech), [Digital & Paid Media](/digital-paid-media), [CPG, Food & Beverage](/cpg)
**Related terms:** [Signal Loss](/glossary/signal-loss), [Retail Media](/glossary/retail-media), [Measurement Fragmentation](/glossary/measurement-fragmentation)

### `/glossary/clinical-trial-communications`
**Clinical Trial Communications**

Specialized communications around the design, recruitment, results, and regulatory submissions of clinical trials for pharmaceuticals, biologics, and medical devices.

Clinical trial communications combines investor relations (trial results move stock prices), medical affairs (engaging investigators and KOLs), patient recruitment (often the rate-limiting step in trial enrollment), and regulatory disclosure (the FDA approval pathway requires specific public communications). Trial readouts are among the highest-stakes communications events in healthcare.

**Where it's used:** [Healthcare & Health Tech](/healthcare), [Investor Relations](/investor-relations)
**Related terms:** [FDA-Regulated Promo](/glossary/fda-regulated-promo)

### `/glossary/coalition-strategy`
**Coalition Strategy**

Public affairs work that organizes multiple aligned organizations — companies, trade associations, advocacy groups, unions — into a coordinated campaign for or against a policy outcome.

Coalition campaigns shape regulatory outcomes, legislation, and political environment in ways that single-organization advocacy cannot. The work combines grassroots organizing, grasstops engagement, earned media, paid media, and coordinated lobbying. Coalition disclosure is increasingly required at the state level and increasingly visible to AI engines through public filings.

**Where it's used:** [Public Affairs & Government](/public-affairs)
**Related terms:** [Lobbying Disclosure](/glossary/lobbying-disclosure), [FARA](/glossary/fara)

### `/glossary/comparison-query`
**Comparison Query**

A prompt to an AI engine asking which option is best among named alternatives — "Snowflake vs. Databricks," "Edelman vs. Weber Shandwick," "Claude vs. ChatGPT for X."

Comparison queries are among the highest-stakes AI retrieval moments in B2B and consumer commerce. The brands cited as the recommended option capture meaningful shortlist position; the brands named only as alternatives still benefit; the brands missing entirely cede the consideration set. Winning comparison queries is one of the most measurable outcomes of GEO.

**Where it's used:** [AI Communications & GEO](/ai), [GEO](/geo), [B2B Tech & SaaS](/b2b)
**Related terms:** [Citation Share](/glossary/citation-share), [GEO](/glossary/geo)

### `/glossary/creator-economy`
**Creator Economy**

The ecosystem of independent creators — across YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Substack, podcasts, Twitch, and emerging platforms — who build audiences and monetize through advertising, brand partnerships, subscriptions, and commerce.

The creator economy has institutionalized. Top creators operate as media companies with teams, agencies, and revenue diversified across multiple platforms. Brand partnerships with creators are now more central to consumer marketing than traditional celebrity endorsements in many categories. AI tools — for content generation, editing, and monetization — are reshaping creator economics.

**Where it's used:** [Influencer & Creator](/influencer-creator), [Social Media](/social-media)

### `/glossary/crisis-velocity`
**Crisis Velocity**

The speed at which a reputational event spreads from initial incident to broad public awareness — now measured in minutes rather than hours.

Crisis velocity has compressed dramatically. Social platforms surface events in minutes, AI summarization speeds opinion formation, and regulators have moved toward shorter disclosure windows. The 24-hour rule has replaced the 72-hour rule across most categories. Disclosure quality is now a primary driver of recovery, alongside incident severity.

**Where it's used:** [Crisis Communications](/crisis-communications), [Cybersecurity](/cyber)
**Related terms:** [24-Hour Rule](/glossary/24-hour-rule), [Disclosure Quality](/glossary/disclosure-quality)

CTV has emerged as the largest growth surface in digital advertising. The leading platforms — Roku, Amazon Fire TV, YouTube TV, Disney+, Netflix's ad tier, Paramount+, and Hulu — compete for ad budgets that used to flow through linear television and digital video. CTV advertising blends the brand-building characteristics of TV with the targeting and measurement capabilities of digital.

### `/glossary/dark-site`
**Dark Site**

A pre-built crisis communications website — held offline in normal operations — that goes live when a major reputation event requires a dedicated information hub.

Dark sites typically include the holding statement, FAQ, executive video, regulatory filings, customer notification details, and updates as the event develops. They centralize information away from the main brand site and signal that the company is operating with discipline rather than improvising. Dark sites are now standard for major airlines, energy companies, financial institutions, and healthcare systems.

Short-duration fantasy sports contests — typically one day, one week, or one game — where users assemble lineups and compete for cash prizes.

DraftKings and FanDuel are the dominant U.S. DFS operators, both having expanded from DFS into sports betting and iGaming. ### `/glossary/disclosure-quality`
**Disclosure Quality**

The clarity, specificity, completeness, and timeliness of a public disclosure — from regulatory filings to crisis communications.

Disclosure quality has emerged as a measurable variable in stock and reputation recovery after corporate crises. Research on SEC 8-K cyber disclosures shows that companies with clear, specific, well-timed disclosures recover stock price faster than companies with vague, delayed, or incomplete disclosures — even when controlling for incident severity. The implication for communications: how a crisis is disclosed now matters as much as what is disclosed.

**Where it's used:** [Crisis Communications](/crisis-communications), [Cybersecurity](/cyber), [Investor Relations](/investor-relations)
**Related terms:** [SEC 8-K](/glossary/sec-8k), [Breach Response](/glossary/breach-response)

### `/glossary/disinformation`
**Disinformation**

False or misleading information deliberately created and distributed to deceive — distinct from misinformation, which is unintentional.

Disinformation campaigns have become a recurring communications challenge for corporations, governments, and public figures. Sources include state-sponsored operations, activist short-sellers, competitor-aligned operations, and AI-generated content. Responding to disinformation requires specialized forensic work, platform engagement, regulatory escalation where appropriate, and disciplined counter-narrative communications.

**Where it's used:** [Crisis Communications](/crisis-communications), [Reputation Management](/reputation-management)
**Related terms:** [Reputation Recovery](/glossary/reputation-recovery), [Activist Short-Seller](/glossary/activist-short-seller)

A software platform that allows advertisers and agencies to buy digital ad inventory programmatically across multiple ad exchanges and publishers.

Leading DSPs include The Trade Desk, Amazon DSP, Google DV360, Yahoo DSP, and Adform. DSPs sit on the buy side of programmatic; SSPs sit on the sell side. DSP consolidation has accelerated as signal loss, walled gardens, and retail media compress the open programmatic ecosystem.

### `/glossary/dtc`

A business model where brands sell directly to end consumers through their own channels — typically e-commerce — rather than through traditional retail intermediaries.

The DTC wave reshaped beauty, CPG, apparel, mattresses, eyewear, and dozens of other categories — from Warby Parker and Casper to Glossier and Allbirds. Many DTC brands have since added retail distribution as customer acquisition cost compressed pure-online economics. The category remains a distinctive communications environment combining performance marketing, creator partnerships, and brand building.

### `/glossary/dual-use-technology`
**Dual-Use Technology**

Technology that has both civilian and military applications — including AI, autonomous systems, advanced manufacturing, biotech, and cybersecurity tools.

Dual-use technology firms operate across military and commercial markets simultaneously, which creates a distinct communications challenge: messaging that resonates with Pentagon procurement, allied governments, commercial buyers, and venture investors all at once. ### `/glossary/earned-media`
**Earned Media**

Press coverage a brand secures through pitching, relationship-building, and the strength of its story — as opposed to paid placement.

Earned media is the citation engine of modern reputation. Press coverage now feeds organic search, social amplification, AI engine training data, structured-data layers, and the entity authority that compounds reputation across every surface. A Tier-1 citation creates a retrieval anchor that AI engines reuse on every subsequent query about the brand.

**Where it's used:** [Earned Media](/earned-media), [GEO](/geo)

### `/glossary/embargo`
**Embargo**

A condition placed on a press release or news item specifying a date and time before which the information cannot be published.

Embargoes give reporters time to write the story properly while ensuring coordinated launch coverage. Broken embargoes are a reputational event in both directions — reporters who break embargoes get cut off from future access, and PR teams that announce embargo violations destroy reporter relationships. Embargoes are typically negotiated with named reporters at named publications, not blasted broadly.

**Where it's used:** [Earned Media](/earned-media)
**Related terms:** [Exclusive](/glossary/exclusive), [Press Release](/glossary/press-release)

### `/glossary/embedded-finance`
**Embedded Finance**

The integration of financial services — payments, lending, insurance, banking — directly inside non-financial brands and platforms.

Examples include Shopify Capital, Uber's banking products, Apple Pay, and the wave of B2B SaaS platforms adding payment processing and lending to their core offerings. Embedded finance is moving banking infrastructure inside customer-facing brands, redrawing the competitive landscape between traditional banks and the platforms now distributing financial products at scale.

**Where it's used:** [Financial Services & Fintech](/finserv), [B2B Tech & SaaS](/b2b)

### `/glossary/entity-authority`
**Entity Authority**

The degree to which AI engines recognize a brand, person, product, or framework as a named, distinct, citable entity.

Entity authority is what makes a brand retrievable inside generative search. It is built through consistent naming across sources, schema markup that explicitly identifies the entity, cross-references to authoritative sources like Wikipedia and Wikidata, and the editorial footprint that reinforces the entity's existence and relevance. Brands with low entity authority appear in AI answers as generic descriptions rather than named recommendations.

**Where it's used:** [GEO](/geo), [AI Communications & GEO](/ai)
**Related terms:** [GEO](/glossary/geo), [Retrieval Anchor](/glossary/retrieval-anchor), [Schema](/glossary/schema)

### `/glossary/exclusive`
**Exclusive**

A story offered to a single reporter or publication before — or instead of — broader distribution.

Exclusives are how major launches, scoops, and crisis stories typically run. The trade is straightforward: the publication gets first access and competitive advantage; the brand gets a more substantial story written by a more invested reporter than a wire blast would produce. Exclusives are a primary tool of Tier-1 placement strategy.

**Where it's used:** [Earned Media](/earned-media)
**Related terms:** [Embargo](/glossary/embargo), [Tier-1 Publication](/glossary/tier-1-publication)

### `/glossary/executive-reputation`
**Executive Reputation**

The public perception of a senior leader — CEO, founder, executive — as a distinct asset from the corporate brand.

Executive reputation now operates as a measurable variable in corporate valuation, recruitment, and crisis exposure. Founder-led brands carry valuation premiums tied directly to founder visibility. CEO communications has moved from quarterly earnings cycles to continuous public presence. Executive reputation work combines bylined writing, podcast presence, social platforms, AI visibility for the named entity, and the editorial footprint that makes the leader retrievable.

**Where it's used:** [Executive & Founder Branding](/executive-founder-branding), [Reputation Management](/reputation-management)

### `/glossary/fara`
**FARA (Foreign Agents Registration Act)**

A U.S. federal law requiring agents acting on behalf of foreign governments, political parties, or principals to disclose their relationship, activities, and payments to the Department of Justice.

FARA has become structurally important to communications and reputation because every FARA filing is now public, searchable, and increasingly ingested by AI engines. The disclosure record shapes how a foreign principal, lobbying firm, or law firm appears inside generative search. Tightening enforcement under DOJ has expanded the scope of activities considered FARA-triggering — particularly around digital, social, and influencer engagement on behalf of foreign principals.

**Where it's used:** [Public Affairs & Government](/public-affairs), [Crisis Communications](/crisis-communications)
**Related terms:** [Lobbying Disclosure](/glossary/lobbying-disclosure), [Foreign Principal](/glossary/foreign-principal)

### `/glossary/fda-regulated-promo`
**FDA-Regulated Promotional Communications**

Communications by pharmaceutical, medical device, and certain healthcare companies that must comply with FDA rules governing what can be claimed, how risks must be disclosed, and how branded vs. unbranded content is structured.

FDA-regulated promo is one of the most constrained communications environments in any industry. Every branded campaign must include fair balance, risk disclosure, and approved indications. Unbranded disease-state education and patient resources operate under different rules. The category requires specialized agency teams and tight medical-legal-regulatory (MLR) review.

**Where it's used:** [Healthcare & Health Tech](/healthcare)
**Related terms:** [Clinical Trial Communications](/glossary/clinical-trial-communications)

### `/glossary/featured-snippet`
**Featured Snippet**

A summarized answer Google extracts from a web page and displays at the top of search results — typically a paragraph, list, or table answering a specific question.

Featured snippets are the original AEO surface, predating AI Overviews by nearly a decade. Winning a featured snippet typically requires answering a clear question directly, structuring the answer to match user intent, and using schema markup to signal answer-readiness. Featured snippets often coexist with AI Overviews on the same query — both surfaces draw from similar signals.

**Where it's used:** [AEO](/aeo), [SEO](/seo)
**Related terms:** [AEO](/glossary/aeo), [AI Overview](/glossary/ai-overview), [Schema](/glossary/schema)

### `/glossary/foreign-principal`
**Foreign Principal**

Under FARA, any foreign government, political party, individual, or organization on whose behalf a U.S.-based agent acts.

The foreign principal designation triggers FARA registration and disclosure obligations. DOJ enforcement around foreign principal activity has tightened significantly — particularly around digital, social, and influencer engagement that wouldn't have been considered FARA-triggering a decade ago. ### `/glossary/schema`
**Schema (Structured Data)**

Schema (using the Schema.org vocabulary, typically rendered in JSON-LD format) is one of the most important signals for GEO, AEO, and modern SEO. Schema-marked content is more accurately retrieved, more confidently cited, and more cleanly parsed into structured AI responses. The most retrieval-relevant schema types include `Article`, `Person`, `Organization`, `Dataset`, `FAQPage`, `BreadcrumbList`, and `DefinedTerm`.

**Where it's used:** [GEO](/geo), [AEO](/aeo), [SEO](/seo)
**Related terms:** [Entity Authority](/glossary/entity-authority), [GEO](/glossary/geo)

### `/glossary/sec-8k`
**SEC 8-K**

A filing public companies must submit to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission within four business days of certain material events — including material cybersecurity incidents, leadership changes, M&A activity, and bankruptcy.

The SEC's 2023 cybersecurity disclosure rules expanded 8-K requirements to mandate disclosure of material cyber incidents — making breach communications a regulatory and reputational event simultaneously. Analysis of 8-K cyber disclosures shows that disclosure quality is a primary driver of stock recovery, alongside incident severity. Clear, specific, and timely 8-K filings now correlate strongly with faster reputation and price recovery after breaches.

### `/glossary/seo`
**SEO (Search Engine Optimization)**

The practice of structuring content, technical infrastructure, and authority signals to earn ranked placement on traditional search results pages.

SEO is the foundation layer of a broader discovery stack that now includes AEO and GEO. The underlying authority signals — structured data, entity recognition, editorial citations, technical site quality — feed all three. Strong SEO infrastructure produces the structured, retrievable, authority-rich content that answer engines pull from; a site with weak technical SEO cannot succeed in GEO or AEO. SEO's role has shifted from "ranking" to "feeding the citation layer."

**Where it's used:** [SEO](/seo), [GEO](/geo), [AEO](/aeo)

### `/glossary/share-of-model`
**Share of Model**

A measurement of how frequently a brand appears across answers from a single AI model relative to category competitors.

Share of model is the per-engine version of citation share — measuring brand presence inside, for example, ChatGPT specifically, rather than across all engines combined. Different models weight different sources, which means share of model can vary widely between ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini for the same brand. Some brands index strongly on Reddit-trained models; others index on press-trained models.

**Where it's used:** [AI Communications & GEO](/ai), [GEO](/geo)
**Related terms:** [Citation Share](/glossary/citation-share), [GEO](/glossary/geo)

### `/glossary/signal-loss`
**Signal Loss**

The reduction in available behavioral and identity data for digital advertising — driven by privacy regulation, platform policy changes, and the deprecation of third-party cookies and mobile identifiers.

Signal loss has pushed advertising budgets toward first-party data, retail media, clean rooms, and contextual targeting. It has also compressed measurement accuracy across programmatic, making measurement fragmentation one of the defining challenges of AdTech. Brands that built audience data programs early have a meaningful advantage; brands that depended on third-party data are still rebuilding.

**Where it's used:** [AdTech & MarTech](/adtech), [Digital & Paid Media](/digital-paid-media)

### `/glossary/source-of-truth`
**Source-of-Truth**

The canonical, authoritative reference for a brand, product, person, or topic — the page or dataset that AI engines treat as the ground-truth definition.

Source-of-truth architecture is core to GEO. For most brands, the source-of-truth should be an owned page (the brand's own site) with schema markup, supplemented by Wikipedia, Wikidata, and authoritative editorial coverage that points back to it. When the source-of-truth is weak or contested, AI engines default to whichever third party fills the gap — often a competitor or a critic.

### `/glossary/sportsbook-handle`
**Sportsbook Handle**

The total dollar amount wagered with a sportsbook over a defined period — typically reported monthly by state regulators.

Handle is the top-of-funnel revenue metric in legal sports betting. State-by-state handle data is published by gaming regulators and aggregated by industry trackers. Handle is the headline number; revenue (gross gaming revenue) is the operator's actual take after payouts. Operators compete intensely for share of state handle in the highest-volume markets.

### `/glossary/training-data`
**Training Data**

The body of text, code, and structured information an AI model is trained on — which shapes what the model knows, what it can cite, and how it summarizes any given topic.

Training data is asymmetric. Different LLMs weight different sources: Reddit features heavily in some, Wikipedia in others, paywalled journalism in still others. The earned media a brand secures becomes part of training data on the next training cycle — which is why high-authority editorial coverage compounds into citation share over time.

### `/glossary/walled-gardens`
**Walled Gardens**

### `/glossary/zero-click-search`
**Zero-Click Search**

A search query that gets resolved on the results page itself — through a featured snippet, knowledge panel, AI Overview, or direct answer — without the user clicking through to any underlying source.

Zero-click search has become the dominant pattern for information-led queries. For publishers and brands, zero-click compresses traffic but elevates citation visibility — the brands cited inside the answer surface get authority signal even without the click. The strategic implication: optimization shifts from driving clicks to winning citations.
